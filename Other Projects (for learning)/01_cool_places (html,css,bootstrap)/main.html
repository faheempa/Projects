10 PLACES TO COOL OFF IN INDIA’S SWELTERING SUMMER HEAT
1.

MANALI

One of the most beautiful places you must visit in India has got to be Manali in Himachal Pradesh. At an elevation of over 2000 meters, this hill station is a well-known tourist destination not only because of its immense beauty but also because of its superb weather. Cool and pleasant in the summer months, with temperatures ranging between 10 and 25 degrees, the town is extremely pleasant.

2.

SHIMLA

Another well-known and frequented hill station in the North is Shimla; with its colonial history and natural beauty, the area is also blessed with great weather. Best visited during the summer months, if you feel like getting out and exploring the rich culture and monuments here, Shimla is also a wonderland for anyone that wants to experience a white winter and enjoys bundling themselves up to have some fun in the snow.

3.

LADAKH

Summer sees the entire Ladakh area come alive with flora and fauna blossoming everywhere you turn. An ideal time for bikers to take the ride of a lifetime, the weather during this season is not only cool and pleasant but also ensures less road closures and weather related delays. Even if your intention is not to get on the road, Ladakh is still best visited in the summer as it makes for a great escape from the blistering hot weather in places like Delhi, Mumbai and other low lying states.

4.

AULI

The coolest place in Indian during the summer months, Auli is a well-known and much loved ski resort in Uttarakhand. At over two and a half thousand feet above sea level, the place is home to the Nanda Devi, Mana Parvat and Kamat Kamet mountain ranges which are snow capped throughout the year, and as such, the region is always pleasant and cool. Host to many winter sport events, Auli has visitors coming from across the globe to enjoy the slopes.

5.

NAINITAL

Merely four hours from Delhi by road, Nainital is a beautiful summer holiday destination, surrounded by rolling hills and blessed with great weather year round. A favourite place for people from Delhi and Chandigarh to visit when the heat gets too much in the capital, Nainital is always bustling with tourists. Even the winter months here are great as the countryside is equally spectacular during this time of year.

6.

RANIKHET

A little hill station cantonment area, Ranikhet is also called the Queens Meadow on account of the fact that this is believed to be one of Queen Padmini of Kumaoni residences. Steeped in ancient history and adorned with beautiful monuments, not to mention the natural magnificence and wonderful climate, makes this town a top contender for summer relief in Uttarakhand.

7.

MOUNT ABU

Rajasthan’s only hill station, Mount Abu is a town steeped in ancient history. With temples and other heritage monuments dating back over a thousand years, it is little wonder that the entire hill station is like a museum and a paradise for history buffs or architecture enthusiasts. Along with a lot of places to see, there is also a lot to do here such as boating, trekking and exploring. Winter months are amazing in Mount Abu but the summer is especially an inviting relief from the desert heat.

8.

KASHMIR

The beauty and splendor of Kashmir is legendary, as is the weather there. If you happen to be feeling the heat and need a break from the city then this is the best place to chill out, literally. With summers in the state seeing temperatures usually between 15 and 25 degrees, the place feels like heaven as soon as you arrive; so much to see and do in the region is another reason Kashmir has got to be high up on the list of cool places to visit in the summer.

9.

MUKTESHWAR

Mukteshwar, a quaint hill station in Uttarakhand, is one of the country’s best summer vacation destinations. Cool weather even during the peak of summer makes for an awesome and relaxing break from the heat and hustle of the city. Boasting lush green forests and some of the region’s best trails for trekking, the cool Himalayan breeze is an added bonus that is awesomely refreshing.

10.

GANGTOK

Meaning hill-top, Gangtok is perched at an elevation of over 1500 meters and is fortunate to have cool weather even in the summer months. This hill station in the North East region of India is not only known for its awesome weather but also for a host of activities and places to see. Nature’s abundance is evident here especially in the summer with the forests coming alive making for some amazing trekking, rafting and camping.
